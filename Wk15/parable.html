+ SEE THE LIVE SITE

The Story I selected was Parable of the Palace. My interpretation of this text was the balance between myth as power and truth as destruction. In my design, I created two perspectives: The Yellow Emperor and The Poet. I divided the story into 6 sections, with each perspective containing 3 parts. I used a grid element as sort of a 'map' of each section and gave the background images of each perspective different saturations to further along the theme of cheery myth vs. darker reality.


EMPEROR GRID



POET GRID







THE EMPEROR'S STORY

I interpreted the Yellow Emperor as a figure that controls the narrative and cut certain elements of the story that did not support his myth of his palace being paradise. I then added an on click function within the certain words of the Emperor's story that changes the inner HTML to show the original text that I cut.










THE POET'S STORY

For the poet, I added an on click function that made certain words disappear or fade away, highlighting how his words had the power to dismantle the illusion of the palace. The experience should be similar in whichever screen size as I tried out the flex box method to make my design responsive. My design is linear if the reader starts from the Yellow Emperor perspective, non-linear if they begin with the poet.
